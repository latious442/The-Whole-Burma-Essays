Home vs Abroad: Which Travel Experience Do You Prefer?

If I were asked, "Would you like to travel around?", I would definitely go for it. As I too have a teenage spirit, I'd love to go on a world tour and expand my knowledge range. Actually, I have passed a pretty long era without traveling, which my curious soul has been craving-a well-planned itinerary, some nostalgic moments. Recently, I have travelled both home and abroad, and I enjoyed my journey so much as I wished. And then there is one confusing question awaiting most of our answers: which one is better, exploring home or abroad? Moreover, some people think that one should visit his own country first before travelling abroad. In my opinion, the reasonable difference between travelling home and abroad makes one's meticulous feelings come up with various experiences.
I prefer to travel home before traveling abroad for many reasons. To travel home, for me, is preferred before venturing abroad for several reasons: first and foremost, while exploring local tourist spots, I am exposed to the rich cultural heritage and natural beauty that my country boasts of. The same case applies to historical places and national parks that enlighten me with a lot about my nation's history and biodiversity. Besides, travel within the country is often rather cheap and more convenient to organize even without much bureaucracy, complicated paperwork, or long flights. This saves a lot of time and resources besides controlling the stress commonly related to traveling. Finally, it supports local tourism and is done through sustainable travel. This is an excellent benefit, whereby I could have an even greater impact on my community because I get to explore my homeland first. Another reason why I am much more inclined towards domestic traveling over international ones is because one gets to connect with the local communities and their traditions. I can see a variance in customs, food, and dialect whenever I visit different regions in my country. For instance, festivals and dishes that one gets to taste may enrich one's perception about one's country's diverse cultural tapestry. The touch with life at the local level uproots feelings of belonging and pride in my nationality. I eventually travelled abroad, as it better equips me to handle challenges thrown up by international travel with much ease. This is particularly important for solo travelers or those who may feel anxious about going too far from home. I can build up my confidence and comfort level gradually by starting with domestic travels before going on to tackle the complexities of international trips.
In short, the above reasons are the evidence showing the preference for traveling within one's own country before traveling abroad. Traveling in one's own country before going to other nations brings many advantages in a nutshell. It enables me, therefore, to link deeply with the cultural and natural wealth of my homeland, associate with local people, and acquire vital traveling experience. Familiarity and safety in domestic travel form a comfortable base, hence being an ideal starting point for any traveler. In case of shifting the focus to local tourism, I contribute positively not only to the fulfillment of my personal experiences but also to my country's economy and sustainable travel practices. Eventually, this approach will lead me to be fully prepared and confident at the time when international adventures call.
