My journey of learning English


Hello, I'm Aung Khant Htoo, an eighteen-year-old learner from Thitpokepin village, Lewe Township. Recently, I completed my Matriculation exam, and I'm currently dedicated to enhancing my English skills. I have a voracious appetite for the English language and have traversed a long and challenging journey in my pursuit of mastery. I'm now updating my current level in a steady and efficient way. In the past, I'd also encountered an impressive grow-up of myself with English, thanks to their unwavering solid supports. I'm here to share my journey of learning English. I hope all of you will find it fascinating and interesting.
Learning English is crucial because it opens doors to many opportunities worldwide. It's the main language used in global communication, making it easier to do business, study, and connect with people from different countries. Knowing English can boost career prospects, as many companies require it for international jobs. It also gives access to a vast amount of information online, helps with travel, and allows understanding of English-speaking cultures through books, movies, and music. In education, English proficiency can lead to scholarships and research opportunities. Plus, it's vital for participating in international events, collaborating on global issues, and staying up-to-date in technology and innovation. Overall, mastering English helps individuals navigate our interconnected world and achieve personal and professional goals. As for me, mastering English is essential for advancing my career and providing better opportunities for my family. It opens doors to higher-paying jobs, international networking, and professional growth. With English proficiency, I can access a broader range of career paths and compete effectively in the global job market. Additionally, it allows me to communicate with colleagues, clients, and partners from different parts of the world, enhancing collaboration and expanding business opportunities. Ultimately, investing in improving my English skills not only benefits my career but also ensures a brighter future for my family.
Actually, I found no interest in English when I was young. What I mean is that it is not more than a subject in the curriculum. Since I was in Kindergarten, I had started my alphabet learning, not knowing what they were and how they were useful. But, I truly enjoyed this learning era. The memorable cacophonies in my primary academy mixed with some vocabularies like A for ‘Apple’. But, I was still showing no obvious appetite in English to assume as an important language in my future. I still remember that I was over the moon when I could memorize two or three sentences and tell my teacher I’d done. In the exam, I only remember that I had to take into account what the teachers gave me and forced to go over again and again. So, it was so rare in my village to acquire proper learning method of English Language. Not only many adults but also number of students liked to study by heart rather than approaching English as a language. In middle school, I stared standing out for my deep passion for English, despite struggling with grammar rules. In Grade 7, I encountered Daw Lay Lay Nwe, a teacher who charged a modest fee of 5,000 MMK per month to teach basic grammar. Under her patient guidance, my understanding of English blossomed, and I began expressing myself with newfound confidence and clarity. This journey ignited my lifelong passion for the language, demonstrating the transformative power of dedicated mentorship and a thirst for learning. But, in spite of my deep emotions emerged, I was still a weak learner with my lack of fluency for four main skills in English. In high school, I also met a person who could illuminate my covered obsession for English. Tr Daw Chit Hsu taught me more advanced gramma and made me practise to improve my reading skills. I could feel transformative development compared to me in middle school. It would be more efficient as I was not a pure greenhorn, thanks to my middle school teacher. But, owing to the Covid-19 pandemic, I stopped my learning as it was really severe throughout the country and schools were forced to close. I was totally jack of all trades during this pandemic and had no thirst to continue my learning. I was really happy to spend time with my friends, creating unforgettable memories. But, my endeavor for this language was back with the reconnection of my teacher, Daw Chit Hsu. It was my first time of learning online and also the first time of interacting in English. My teacher told me that our journey of learning English in language form had started. At the commencement, I got into hot water in speaking and listening. But, I was gradually well-established with it. Also, my teacher made me write in my forms- sentences, paragraphs, and essays. Owing to a well-built teacher-student relationship, I found myself among obvious progresses and I was so pleased with my growth. We together spoke in English so as to improve my speaking. I tried my best to comprehend what teacher said for my boost of listening. One or two essays per week could absolutely make me think and write properly. In these ways, I learned to enhance my English on the wrong side of 1 year. When I was G-12, I lacked my successive learning since I had to focus on my school lessons. But, now, after my matriculation exam, I restarted my language learning again and decided to level up. I have come through my long journey and I haven’t found any prime time yet. So, it’s the time to prepare to see my prime version. I am now practising four skills and advanced gramma for my entrance of IELTS exam. I sometimes visit free websites and learn new vocabularies, practise listening and read articles. All things considered, I’m just an intermediate level learner with strong passion and try in every way to promote myself for my coming opportunities and life welfare.
